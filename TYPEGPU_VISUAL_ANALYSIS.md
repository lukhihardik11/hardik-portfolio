- The track shows **38%** text embossed/engraved into the metallic surface
- There's a **contact shadow** beneath the entire slider assembly

- The stick/pin is a simple **gray metallic rod**
- The gel cube has **visible thickness** - you can see through it partially

## Key Material Properties Observed

### Translucency
- Both switch and slider show genuine translucency - you can partially see through the gel material
- Light passes through and creates internal color variations

### Subsurface Scattering
- Light enters the gel and scatters internally, creating soft internal glow
- Most visible in the blue knob where white highlights appear on top

### Contact Shadows
- Objects cast soft, diffused shadows on the surface below
- Shadows are not hard-edged but soft and spread

### Colored Light Spill
- The gel material emits/transmits colored light onto nearby surfaces
- Blue glow under the switch knob, teal glow next to the slider

### Volume Preservation
- The gel shapes maintain volume - they look like they have mass
- Even when deformed, the total volume appears conserved

### Rim/Edge Highlights
- Thin bright highlights along edges suggest glass-like refraction
- These are not heavy borders but subtle light catches

### Recessed Track
- The slider track appears recessed into the surface, like a machined channel
- This creates a sense of physical depth and integration with the surface

### 3D Protrusion
- The knob sits ABOVE the track surface, creating genuine 3D depth
- This is not achieved with box-shadow alone but with actual visual volume
